Pillars of Eternity II: Deadfire


Pillars of Eternity II: Deadfire is an isometric RPG set in the fantasy world of Eora, where players embark on a seafaring adventure across the Deadfire Archipelago. As the Watcher, a hero with the ability to perceive and interact with souls, players must navigate treacherous waters, forge alliances, and uncover ancient mysteries.

Pillars of Eternity II: Deadfire, developed by Obsidian Entertainment, is a masterclass in classic RPG storytelling and immersive world-building, offering players a rich and vibrant fantasy experience set in the expansive world of Eora. Building upon the foundation laid by its predecessor, Deadfire takes players on an epic seafaring adventure across the exotic and mysterious Deadfire Archipelago. At the heart of Deadfire is its deep and engaging narrative, which weaves together themes of exploration, politics, and spirituality in a rich tapestry of intrigue and adventure. As the Watcher, players are tasked with unraveling the mysteries of the Archipelago, confronting ancient gods, and shaping the fate of nations through their choices and actions. Gameplay in Deadfire is a seamless blend of real-time exploration and tactical combat, offering players a wealth of options for customizing their party, mastering powerful abilities, and overcoming formidable challenges. Whether engaging in intense naval battles, delving into ancient ruins, or forging alliances with colorful characters, every decision feels meaningful and impactful, shaping the course of the player's journey. One of Deadfire's standout features is its dynamic world, which reacts to the player's choices and actions in meaningful ways. From the bustling port cities of Neketaka to the uncharted islands of the Deadfire, every location is teeming with life and opportunity, inviting players to explore, interact, and uncover the secrets that lie hidden beneath the surface. While Deadfire received praise for its deep storytelling, complex characters, and strategic gameplay, some players criticized its pacing and difficulty spikes, particularly in the later stages of the game. However, these minor flaws do little to detract from the overall experience of immersing oneself in the rich and immersive world of Pillars of Eternity II: Deadfire, a must-play for fans of classic RPGs and epic adventures alike.
